Renewable Energy Fact Sheet

Overview

Renewable energy is derived from sources that are naturally replenished. Unlike fossil fuels, these sources have a lower environmental impact and can be sustained over the long term.

Types of Renewable Energy

  • Solar Energy
  • Wind Energy
  • Hydropower
  • Geothermal Energy
  • Biomass

Key Statistics

Global Renewable Energy Capacity: As of 2022, renewable energy accounts for over 26% of global electricity generation.

Solar Power: The solar energy industry has grown at an average rate of 49% per year over the last decade.

Wind Power: Wind energy contributes to more than 10% of the total global electricity demand.

Environmental Impact

Renewable energy sources produce significantly fewer greenhouse gas emissions compared to traditional fossil fuels, helping to combat climate change and reduce air pollution.

Benefits

  • Reduced Greenhouse Gas Emissions
  • Energy Independence
  • Job Creation in the Renewable Energy Sector
  • Sustainable and Long-Term Energy Solution
